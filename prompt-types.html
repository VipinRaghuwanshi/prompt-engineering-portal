Types of Prompts

Prompt engineering includes several techniques that help guide AI models toward producing accurate and useful responses. Each prompting method provides a different way of instructing the AI to perform tasks. Understanding these prompt types helps users communicate more effectively with AI systems.

Zero-Shot Prompting

The AI performs a task without being provided with examples. It relies only on its pre-trained knowledge.

Few-Shot Prompting

The AI is given a small number of examples before completing a task. These examples help guide the response.

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

The AI explains its reasoning step-by-step, improving performance on complex problems.

Zero-Shot Prompting

Zero-shot prompting is a technique where the AI model is asked to perform a task without any examples. The model relies only on its existing knowledge to generate an answer. This approach is useful for simple tasks where the instructions are clear and the model already understands the topic.

Example:

Translate the sentence:
"Hello world" into French.

Few-Shot Prompting

Few-shot prompting provides the AI with a small number of examples before asking it to perform a task. These examples guide the model and help it understand the expected format of the response.

Example:

English → Spanish
Hello → Hola
Good Morning → Buenos días

Translate: Thank you → ?

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Chain-of-thought prompting encourages the AI model to explain its reasoning step-by-step. This method is especially useful for tasks involving logical reasoning or mathematical calculations.

Example:

Solve step-by-step:
If a train travels 60 km in 1 hour, how far will it travel in 5 hours?

Role-Based Prompting

Role-based prompting instructs the AI to behave as a specific role such as a teacher, programmer, doctor, or mentor. This technique helps generate responses from a particular perspective and improves the quality of explanations.

Example:

Act as a mathematics teacher and explain the concept of fractions to a 10-year-old student.
